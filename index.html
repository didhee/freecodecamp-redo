The Black Panther Lives On.

Here's a time-line of the life of Chadwick Boseman:

  • 1976?? - Born in Anderson, South Carolina, United States.
  • 1995 - Graduated from T.L. Hanna High School where he played on the basketball team. He also placed eight in Original Oratory at a national speech and debate competition.
  • 1998 - He attended, with other classmates, the Oxford Summer Program of the British American Drama Academy at Baliol College, Oxford, which they had been accepted with Denzel Washington being a prominent sponsor.
  • 2000 - He graduated with a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Directing from Howard University, Washington D.C. He was named a Drama League Directing Fellow.
  • 2003 - He was cast in his first television role, an episode of Third Watch, and began playing Reggie Montgomery in the daytime soap opera All My Children. He was fired after voicing out his concerns to producers about racist stereotypes in the script.
  • 2004 - His best-known play, Deep Azure, was commissioned by the Congo Square Theatre Company, Chicago.
  • 2007 - He directed, wrote and produced the short film Broken Pawn.
  • 2008 - His short film was honoured at the Hollywood Black Film Festival.
  • 2010 - He landed his first regular role in the television series Persons Unknown as the Marine Graham McNair.
  • 2013 - He played the lead role in the film, 42, as the baseball legend Jackie Robinson.
  • 2016 - He began portraying the Marvel Comic character T'Challa/Black Panther in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Captain America: Civil War was his first film in a five-picture deal with Marvel Entertainment. He was diagnosed with stage III colon cancer, which he never disclosed nor publicly spoke about.
  • 2017 - He portrayed Thurgod Marshall in the biographical film Marshall.
  • 2018 - Boseman returned as the Black Panther in the Black Panther movie which focused on the character and his home country, Wakanda, in Africa. The film opened to great anticipation, becoming one of the highest-grossing films.
  • 2019 - He starred in 21 Bridges, an American action thriller, as an NYPD detective who shuts down the eponymous twenty-one bridges of Manhatta to find two suspected cop killers.
  • 2020 - Boseman starred in the Netflix films, da 5 Bloods and Ma Rainey's Black Bottom. At this time, his cancer had already progressed to stage IV, but he continued to work. He died at his home as a result of complications related to colon cancer on August 28, 2020, with his wife and family by his side.
